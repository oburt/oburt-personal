In the fall of 2008, I uprooted myself and moved out to the biggest city of them all, New York. I came to NYU to study accounting at the Leonard N. Stern School of Business. And just so we're clear, accounting does not just equal bookkeeping. I like to think of it more as financial statement analysis or monetary forensics. I won't harp on it too much, but as a degree basis for all-encompassing business knowledge and problem-solving, accounting is hard to beat.

While I attended NYU to study business, before I knew it, I had somehow already completed a minor in computer science by the end of my sophomore year.

I've been coding off & on since I was nine years old, but thank god university finally taught me real object-oriented programming and proper data structures. I guess once technology is in your blood though, you never really lose it (cyborg pun intended).
