In the fall of 2008, I uprooted myself and moved out to the biggest city of them all, New York. I came to NYU to study accounting at the Leonard N. Stern School of Business. And just so we're clear, accounting does not just equal bookkeeping. I like to think of it more as financial statement analysis or monetary forensics.

While I attended NYU to study business, before I knew it, I had somehow already completed a minor in computer science by the end of my sophomore year. I've been coding off & on since I was nine years old, but thank god university finally taught me real object-oriented programming and proper data structures. I guess once technology is in your blood though, you never really lose it (cyborg pun intended).

I was fortunate enough to study abroad in Praha, Česká Republika in the fall of 2010. I certainly couldn't do it justice in a paragraph of words, but suffice it to say, Prague is the sort of place that has a way of charming and changing everyone who visits. As a popular Kafka quote reads, "Prague never lets you go...this dear little mother has sharp claws."
