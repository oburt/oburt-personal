In the fall of 2008, I uprooted myself and moved out to the biggest city of them all, New York. I came to NYU to study accounting at the Leonard N. Stern School of Business. And just so we're clear, accounting != bookkeeping. Accounting often is more accurately described as financial statement analysis, monetary forensics, or just "the language of business." As a degree basis for all-encompassing business knowledge and problem-solving, accounting is hard to beat.

  • Want to know how your competitors are doing? Read their 10-K.
  • Want to understand where your business' money is really going and coming from? Check the cashflow statement.
  • Want to solve problems with real numbers and real ratios?
  • Want to be well-versed in the language that all businesses and pusiness people understand and know all of the above? Accounting.
