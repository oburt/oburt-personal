I co-founded the company formerly known as enjoyn.me in January of 2010. We saw the emerging power of social+local+mobile technologies (e.g. Facebook, Foursquare, and smartphones) and knew those things could help bring people together in the real world.

We launched "SpotOn — Rate. Share. Go." at TechCrunch Disrupt NYC 2011. It was a novel way to use location check-ins to help people figure out new restaurants to visit. At that time, I was working extensively on recommendation algorithms and our python & C back-end.

‹ ›

Following a summer of testing, we realized that when it comes to food, people trust people, not computers. Especially if those people are looking to share or discover "bite-sized restaurant reviews." Thus, we evolved into "Fondu — Dining Journal" in the fall of 2011. We also closed a seed round of $575k.

‹ ›

With Fondu, my focus turned to marketing, product management, and data science.

By May of 2012, we had given awards to top restaurants at SXSW and served free food-truck meals in Greenwish Village. Apple had featured us in the App Store as "New & Noteworthy" and highlighted us as an example of excellent user interface design at WWDC.

Summer was a whirlwind of new product design, exploring where Fondu could go next.

In an unexpected twist, this exploration led into our acquisition by Airbnb (!) announced in the fall of 2012. Most of the team joined Airbnb in SF to improve offline discovery on an entirely new & awesome scale. I, however, still wanted to have a few more NYC adventures.
