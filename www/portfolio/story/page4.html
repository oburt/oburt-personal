I co-founded the company formerly known as enjoyn.me in January of 2010. We saw the emerging power of social+local+mobile technologies (e.g. Facebook, Foursquare, and smartphones) and knew those things could help bring people together in the real world.

We launched "SpotOn — Rate. Share. Go." on-stage at TechCrunch Disrupt NYC 2011. We had developed a way to use location check-ins and ratings to help you and your friends figure out new restaurants to visit. At that time, I was working extensively on recommendation algorithms and our python & C back-end.

‹ ›

Following a summer of user feedback and testing, we realized that when it comes to food, people trust people, not computers. Especially if those people are looking to share or discover "bite-sized restaurant reviews." Thus, we evolved into "Fondu — Dining Journal" in the fall of 2011. We also closed a seed round of $575k.

‹ ›

With our shift away from recommendation engines, my focus turned toward marketing, product management, and data science.

By May of 2012, we had given awards to top restaurants at SXSW and served free food-truck meals in Greenwish Village. Apple had featured us in the App Store as "New and Noteworthy" and then highlighted us as an example of excellent user interface design at WWDC.

That summer was a whirlwind of new product design as we explored what frontiers Fondu could move on to next. Those winds of change turned into our acquisition by Airbnb (!) announced in the fall of 2012. Most of the team joined Airbnb in SF to improve offline discovery on an entirely new & awesome scale. I, however, still wanted to have a few more NYC adventures.
